# ART_BIBLE.md

## Concept: The Hearth of the Cosmos (Tiny Universe)

GemVerse is not a Match-3 game; it is a tiny, living universe. The player lives inside a small, cozy exploration spaceship. The visual identity must feel handcrafted, warm, and lived-in.

---

## SECTION 1: LIVING SHIP
The ship is a "Tiny Home" in deep space, not a military vessel.

*   **Exterior**: A rounded, organic shape—resembling a polished river stone or a walnut shell. It features soft, warm exterior lights and a single, large observation porthole.
*   **Interior**: Lined with wood-grain panels, brass fittings, and plush fabric cushions. It feels like a space-faring cabin.
*   **Rooms**: A central living space that serves as the command center, the kitchen corner, and the research bay. Everything is within arm's reach.
*   **Lighting**: Soft, amber-hued indirect lighting. No harsh overheads; lamps and warm screen glows provide the primary illumination.
*   **Materials**: Polished copper, worn leather, brushed matte plastic, and soft textiles. 
*   **Furniture**: A reclined captain's chair, a circular rug, and modular storage units covered in personal stickers and travel patches.
*   **Windows**: A large, central porthole is the focal point, framed by heavy curtains that can be pulled back.
*   **Atmosphere**: Quiet, intimate, and secure. It feels like a sanctuary amidst the vastness.

---

## SECTION 2: LIVING WORLD RULES
*   **Nothing is static**: Every screen features ambient movement.
*   **Soft Motion**: Lights pulse like a heartbeat; dust motes drift in the gravity-light cabin.
*   **Tactile Feedback**: Every piece of technology features subtle, rhythmic vibration or mechanical movement.

---

## SECTION 3: CREW LIFE
The crew are inhabitants of the ship, not interface elements.

*   **Maintenance Robot**: A boxy, tripod-legged bot that polishes the floor or tightens loose bolts.
*   **Cooking Robot**: A multi-armed unit that spends time near the coffee machine, often whistling steam.
*   **Pet (Space Axolotl)**: A small, bioluminescent creature in a floating tank that drifts toward the player when they are near.
*   **Routine**: They walk between stations, perform idle repairs, or look out the window. When the player mines, they watch with excitement or cheer if a rare crystal is discovered.

---

## SECTION 4: WINDOW TO SPACE
The view outside is a slowly changing masterpiece.

*   **Examples**: Distant nebula clouds (slowly rotating), a gentle meteor shower (faint sparks in the distance), or the silhouette of an ancient, moss-covered satellite passing by.
*   **Pacing**: Everything moves at a glacial pace to encourage the player to stop and "look out the window."

---

## SECTION 5: CRYSTAL MINING MACHINE
The Match-3 board is a physical piece of machinery.

*   **Mechanism**: A circular containment glass where crystals tumble. Mechanical arms on the exterior nudge the crystals. 
*   **Function**: Energy tubes pulse with color when matches occur. Piston-like shutters open/close to receive new crystals.
*   **Authenticity**: The machine produces faint, satisfying "clunk-whir" sounds and emits heat-haze from vents during long mining sessions.

---

## SECTION 6: AMBIENT ANIMATION
*   Coffee machine venting periodic steam.
*   The Captain's chair rocking slowly.
*   Monitor screens flickering with data streams that aren't quite readable.
*   Tiny sparks from a loose wire being fixed by a drone.
*   Blinking status LEDs on the mining machine's pressure gauges.

---

## SECTION 7: EMOTIONAL DESIGN
The player should feel:
*   **Safe**: The ship is a warm shelter.
*   **Cozy**: A sense of "hygge" in deep space.
*   **Curious**: A gentle pull to see what is outside.
*   **Wonder**: The feeling of being small in a beautiful, vast universe.

---

## SECTION 8: VISUAL STORYTELLING
*   **Environmental Cues**: Stacks of worn books near the chair suggest a reader. 
*   **Artifacts**: Strange, non-crystal items on shelves suggest previous discoveries.
*   **Progression**: As the ship "upgrades," add more personal items (a plant growing, a new mug, a map on the wall).

---

## SECTION 9: DESIGN RULES
*   **NO Harsh Neon**: Use warm, diffused light sources only.
*   **NO Plastic Crystals**: Crystals must have deep, multifaceted textures that look mineral and heavy.
*   **Handcrafted Feel**: Every edge should have a slight "worn" quality; nothing should look factory-perfect.
*   **Warm Tech**: All technology must feel analog, heavy, and reliable.
